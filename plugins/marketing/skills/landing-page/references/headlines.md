# Headlines, Preheads, and Deck Copy

Templates, formulas, and worked examples for Step 6 of the sales page framework. Load this when it's time to write the headline.

---

## Prehead — 5 options

The prehead sits above the main headline. It's the first line a reader sees. Its job is to qualify the audience or set up the big promise.

### Option A: Call out the audience

Direct targeting. Works when you can name the ICP in 2-5 words.

- "For copywriters who hate selling..."
- "Attention: dog owners with anxious pets..."
- "For women over 40 struggling to lose weight..."
- "If you run a SaaS company..."
- "For freelancers tired of feast-or-famine income..."
- "Coaches and consultants only:"
- "For solo founders doing $5k-$25k MRR..."

### Option B: Call out the problem

Names the pain directly. Works when the problem is more specific than the audience.

- "Struggling to get clients?"
- "Copy not converting?"
- "Tired of posting content that gets ignored?"
- "Can't seem to grow past $10k months?"
- "Overwhelmed by all the AI tools out there?"
- "Feel like everyone else 'gets it' except you?"
- "Working 60-hour weeks and still broke?"

### Option C: Lead with proof or credibility

Works when the author or the offer has public traction worth naming upfront.

- "From the creator of [known product]..."
- "The system behind $50M in sales..."
- "What 3,000+ students already know..."
- "From a 16-year direct-response copywriter..."
- "The exact method used by [company] to scale past $1M ARR..."

### Option D: Create urgency

Works for time-boxed promotions only. Don't fake urgency — readers catch it.

- "Doors close Friday..."
- "Only 50 spots available..."
- "Early bird pricing ends tonight..."
- "Last chance before the price doubles..."
- "Enrollment closes at midnight EST..."

### Option E: Set up the main headline

Minimal prehead. Lets the main headline carry the weight.

- "Finally..."
- "Announcing..."
- "New:"
- "Discover..."
- "Introducing..."

### Combinations

Most strong preheads stack two of the above.

- "For freelancers tired of feast-or-famine income... Finally:"
- "Attention coaches: doors close Friday"
- "From a 16-year copywriter... New:"
- "For solo SaaS founders stuck under $20k MRR... Announcing:"

## Main headline — the four pieces

Formula:

```
How to go from [where they are now] to [where they want to be] in [timeframe] — without [objection] / even if [limiting belief]
```

### Piece 1 — Where they are now (painful current state)

Specific. Uses their words. Pulled from the ICP context.

- "clueless about copy"
- "stuck at $5k months"
- "posting content that nobody sees"
- "confused by all the AI hype"
- "struggling to get your first client"
- "working 60 hours a week"
- "terrified of selling"
- "losing customers you can't explain"
- "drowning in unqualified leads"

### Piece 2 — Where they want to be (desired outcome)

The transformation. Concrete enough to picture.

- "writing high-converting sales pages"
- "hitting consistent $20k months"
- "building an audience that actually buys"
- "using AI to 10x your output"
- "fully booked 3 months in advance"
- "working 4-day weeks"
- "closing deals without feeling sleazy"
- "shipping products in days, not quarters"
- "running your agency without being in every call"

### Piece 3 — How fast (specific timeframe)

Specific wins. "Soon" loses.

- "in less than 2 hours"
- "in the next 7 days"
- "within 30 days"
- "in one afternoon"
- "by next Friday"
- "in a single weekend"
- "in your first 90 days"

### Piece 4 — Handle the knee-jerk objection

Two frames:

**"without" — external obstacles:**
- "without spending money on ads"
- "without a huge audience"
- "without hiring a team"
- "without writing a single line of code"
- "without quitting your day job"
- "without cold outreach"

**"even if" — internal limiting beliefs:**
- "even if you can barely string a sentence together"
- "even if you've failed before"
- "even if you're starting from zero"
- "even if you hate technology"
- "even if you're an introvert who hates selling"
- "even if you've tried everything else"

## Alternative headline formulas

Use these when "How to go from X to Y" fights the offer shape.

### The Promise Headline

```
[Outcome] in [Timeframe]
```

Examples:
- "Fully booked in 30 days"
- "Your first SaaS customer in 14 days"
- "A 10,000-person list in 90 days"

### The Problem Headline

```
Why [Problem] (And What To Do About It)
```

Examples:
- "Why Your Landing Page Isn't Converting (And What To Do About It)"
- "Why Most SaaS Onboarding Fails (And The 3-Step Fix)"
- "Why Your Welcome Email Is Killing Your List"

### The Story Headline

```
How [Person] [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe]
```

Examples:
- "How One Solo Founder Built a $1M ARR SaaS in 18 Months"
- "How a Burned-Out Designer Booked $80K in Client Work Last Quarter"
- "How a First-Time Course Creator Launched to $47K in 5 Days"

### The Intrigue Headline

```
The [Adjective] [Method/Thing] That [Result]
```

Examples:
- "The Boring Email Sequence That Doubled Our Trial Conversions"
- "The 3-Question Discovery Call That Closes 70% of Leads"
- "The Ugly Landing Page That Outperforms Every A/B Test We've Run"

## Deck copy — the mechanism + proof formula

Deck copy sits below the main headline. It explains HOW you deliver the promise and WHY they should believe you.

Formula:

```
Using [mechanism/method name] that [proof of results]
```

### The mechanism (your unique approach)

- "my proven 5-step framework"
- "the 'Client Magnet' system"
- "a little-known traffic source"
- "my 'Lazy Launch' method"
- "the exact process I use"
- "the 3-pillar approach I built over 10 years"
- "a counterintuitive approach to [topic]"

### The proof (evidence it works)

- "that's generated nine figures in sales"
- "that's helped 370+ professionals get fully booked"
- "responsible for $50M in course sales"
- "that took our pilot group from $0 to $30k/month in 8 months"
- "used by companies like [3 recognizable names]"
- "refined across 500+ launches"
- "with a 60% completion rate across 2,000 students"

### Full deck copy examples

```
Using my proven 5-step framework that's generated nine figures in sales in markets from home services to software

```
