# Close — Price, Guarantee, CTA, FAQ

Step 4 in writing order. Section 5 on the page. The close converts fence-sitters.

Load when it's time to write the close.

---

## Close structure — 4 parts

### Part A — Price justification

**Four micro-steps:**

1. **Value anchor** — what this normally costs, what others pay, what the result is worth
2. **State the price** — exact number, clearly
3. **Explain why that price** — what's your reasoning
4. **Trivialize it** — compare to something they already spend on without thinking

### Part B — Guarantee

A good guarantee removes risk and resells the benefits.

**Elements:**

1. Give it a name — makes it memorable, signals thought went into it
2. Make it longer than expected — 60 days beats 30, 365 if you can stand it
3. Resell the benefits inside the guarantee language
4. Stay positive — focus on their success, not the refund
5. Go beyond just money when possible — extra time, extra coaching, extra asset
6. Make refunds easy — no hoops, no questions, no hassle
7. No fine print

### Part C — CTA

**Elements:**

- Brief recap of what they're getting
- If/then structure ("If you're ready to X, click below")
- Button text that's a benefit, not "Buy Now"

**Button text that works:**
- "Yes, I Want [Benefit]"
- "Get Instant Access"
- "Join The Workshop"
- "Save My Spot"
- "Enroll Now — $[price]"
- "Start My Free Trial"
- "Claim My Seat"

**Button text that fails:**
- "Buy Now"
- "Submit"
- "Purchase"
- "Sign Up"

### Part D — FAQ

5–7 questions that handle remaining objections.

**Rules:**
- First question sums up the offer
- Last question restates the guarantee
- Answers stay 2–4 sentences
- Standard slots: what is it / who it's for / how it's different / what if I don't have [time/money/experience] / when should I expect results / what if it doesn't work
- For long FAQs (7+ questions), add a CTA button in the middle

## FAQ — standard questions

Not every page needs all of these. Pick 5–7 that hit the biggest objections for this offer.

### What exactly is this?

Sums up the offer one more time.

```
Q: What exactly is this?

A: A 4-week self-paced program where you'll learn the exact 5-part sales page framework — with templates, worked examples, and a weekly office-hours call. You'll walk away with at least one finished sales page you can use immediately.
```

### Is this for me?

Lets them self-identify.

```
Q: Who is this for?

A: Course creators, coaches, and service providers who need to write their own sales copy — and people tired of "copywriting advice" that never adds up to a finished page. If you've got an offer but a blank doc where the sales page should be, this is for you.
```

### How is this different?

Differentiates from alternatives.

```
Q: How is this different from other copywriting courses?

A: Most courses teach copy principles. This teaches a specific writing order — story first, headline last — that prevents the "blank page" problem most courses ignore. And every module ends with a working template, not just theory.
```

### What if I don't have [time / money / experience]?

Handles the biggest objections.

```
Q: What if I've never written a sales page before?

A: This was built for first-timers. The backwards writing process was specifically designed so beginners don't get stuck on the headline in the first 5 minutes. If you can follow a template, you can do this.
```

```
Q: How much time does this take?

A: About 4 hours total to go through the training. Plus the time to write your own page (usually 3–8 hours for a first one). You could watch it all in a weekend and have a finished page by the following Friday.
```

### When can I expect results?

Sets expectations.

```
Q: When can I expect to see results?

A: Most people finish their first page within 2 weeks of starting. Conversion results depend on what you're selling and where your traffic comes from — but the framework alone typically outperforms the random-template approach by 30–50% on first launch.
```

### What if it doesn't work?

Restates the guarantee.

```
Q: What if it doesn't work for me?

A: That's what the 60-day "No Questions Asked" Guarantee is for. If you go through the program and don't feel confident writing sales pages, email us and we'll refund your full investment. No questions asked.
```

## Close gotchas

1. **Stating the price with no anchor.** A price without context sounds expensive. Anchor first, state second, trivialize third.

2. **Generic guarantees.** "100% money back" is not a guarantee — it's required by the platform. Name it. Add stakes. Rebuild trust.

3. **"Buy Now" buttons.** Every sales page uses those words. Stand out with a benefit statement.

4. **FAQ with only 3 questions.** Too short signals no real objections were considered. 5–7 is the sweet spot.

5. **FAQ that doesn't restate the guarantee.** Put the guarantee question last. Last thing they read before the final CTA should be the risk reversal.

6. **One CTA at the end of the whole page.** Readers don't scroll. Minimum: one CTA after the offer section, one inside the close, one after the FAQ. Long pages benefit from 4–5 buttons scattered throughout.

7. **Fake urgency.** "Only 3 spots left!" when there are 50 will get caught. Use real urgency (cohort start dates, price increases you actually honor) or skip it.
