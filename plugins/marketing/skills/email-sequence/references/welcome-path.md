# Welcome Path — Full Breakdown

The 7-email sequence that takes a new subscriber from "just downloaded something" to "ready to buy."

---

## Email 1: Hand Off (send immediately)

**What it does:** Delivers the freebie, gives them one quick action they can take in 5 minutes, sets expectations for upcoming emails, and asks for a micro-engagement (reply).

**Subject line ideas:**
- "[Freebie name] is here"
- "Your [freebie] + how to start"
- "Here's [the thing you asked for]"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Greeting | Keep it casual. One line. |
| Delivery | Link to the freebie. Make it obvious. |
| Quick start | One action they can take in the next 5 minutes |
| Expectations | What emails are coming over the next week or two |
| Micro-CTA | Ask them to reply with something specific |
| Sign off | Short and personal |

---

## Email 2: Bond (Day 2)

**What it does:** Builds rapport through a real story. Vulnerability and shared experience. Makes you a person, not a brand.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "Why I made [freebie]"
- "The mistake behind this"
- "Quick story about [topic]"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Story hook | A specific moment or realization — not a generic intro |
| The struggle | What you went through that led to creating this |
| The insight | What you learned from the struggle |
| The connection | How this relates to their situation |
| Forward tease | Hint at what's coming in the next email |
| Sign off | |

---

## Email 3: Teach (Day 4)

**What it does:** Delivers a useful lesson. Demonstrates expertise. Gives them a quick win they can use today.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "The [X] mistake everyone makes"
- "Try this: [specific tactic]"
- "What [person] discovered about [topic]"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Hook | An insight or observation that grabs attention |
| The problem | What most people get wrong about this |
| The fix | What to do instead — tactical, specific, actionable |
| Proof | An example showing this working in real life |
| Action step | One thing they can do right now |
| Sign off | |

---

## Email 4: Teach Again (Day 6)

**What it does:** Same purpose as email 3 — build trust through value. Different angle or topic.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "[Number] things that [outcome]"
- "The question I hear most"
- "This shifted how I think about [topic]"

Same structure as email 3. Different content.

---

## Email 5: Gap (Day 8)

**What it does:** Shows the distance between where they are now (with the freebie) and where they could be (with the paid thing). Introduces the concept of the offer without pitching.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "You can [do X] now. But can you [do Y]?"
- "The step most people skip"
- "What [freebie] doesn't cover"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Acknowledge | What they can now do thanks to the freebie |
| Reveal the gap | What they still can't do |
| Paint the picture | What becomes possible with the full solution |
| Soft mention | The offer exists — no hard sell, just a mention |
| Sign off | |

---

## Email 6: Gentle Ask (Day 10)

**What it does:** Formally introduces the offer. Explains what it is, who it's for, and who it's not for. Lets them self-select.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "The full system (if you want it)"
- "Should you get [product]? Here's how to tell."
- "Not for everyone"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Transition | Connect back to the gap email |
| The offer | What it is and what's included |
| Who it's for | Specific situations where this makes sense |
| Who it's NOT for | Disqualify the wrong buyers |
| Social proof | If you have it |
| Soft CTA | Link to buy — no urgency, no pressure |
| Sign off | |

---

## Email 7: Direct Ask (Day 12)

**What it does:** Makes the clear pitch. Handles the last remaining objection. Creates urgency only if it's real.

**Subject line ideas:**
- "Last thing about [product]"
- "[Product] — yes or no?"
- "Quick decision"

**Structure:**

| Section | What to write |
|---------|--------------|
| Direct opener | No buildup. Get to the point. |
| Core value | Restate what they get in one sentence |
| Final objection | Handle the big one that's still holding people back |
| Urgency | Only if real — price going up, bonus expiring, limited spots |
| Clear CTA | Exactly what to do and where to click |
| Personal note | One final human sentence |
| Sign off | |
