## About Solecooler

Solecooler is a French deep-tech company that makes **heated and cooled insoles that run on no batteries and no chemicals** — only on the energy of your own footsteps. Founded in 2018 in **Perpignan, France** by **Bruno Aubert**, a thermal and materials engineer with 40+ years of experience (founder, CEO, and CTO). The company designs, manufactures, and packages its products in France, and has opened a US subsidiary in **San Diego** to serve the North American market, with a further New York office planned.

The core promise is simple: feet that stay comfortable in extreme conditions, with zero running cost, zero recharging, and zero CO2 in use. The insoles are reversible (one side warms, the other cools), washable, and recyclable.

Three things drive every Solecooler product:

1. **Comfort in extreme temperatures** — keep feet warm in the cold and cool in the heat, across a wider range than any battery system covers.
2. **Sustainability** — no batteries, no chemicals, no CO2 during use; the materials are recyclable, and the company manufactures in France.
3. **Health** — the same step-powered platform extends from everyday comfort (ClimFeet) into medical prevention (WarnFeet, for diabetic foot ulcers).

Solecooler is a member of the **Eurobiomed** health-innovation cluster and is active with the **French-American Chamber of Commerce (SoCal)**.

## The Technology (shared across all products)

Everything Solecooler sells rests on one patented idea: **turn the energy of walking into heating or cooling**, using thermodynamics instead of electronics.

- **Hundreds of miniature heat pumps.** Each insole holds a grid of small air-filled cells (around 260 in a ClimFeet insole) paired as rounded and hexagonal chambers.
- **The Carnot cycle, under your foot.** When you press down, air is forced from the rounded cell into the hexagonal cell and heats up under pressure (like the barrel of a bike pump). When you lift your foot, the hyper-elastic material springs back, the air expands, and it cools (like the air escaping a deflating tire).
- **Reversible.** Flip the insole over to choose the effect: warm side up in winter, cool side up in summer.
- **No batteries, no chemicals, no CO2 in use.** The only power source is your steps. Nothing to charge, nothing to replace, nothing to throw away after one use.
- **Recyclable by design.** The TPU is assembled without glue, so the material can be fully recovered and recycled when an insole is returned for replacement.
- **Copper-enhanced thermal conductivity.** Copper is blended into the material (around 5% in ClimFeet, up to 10% in ClimFeet+) to spread heat and cold more evenly.

**One-line positioning:** the only insoles that both heat *and* cool, powered entirely by your own footsteps — no batteries, no chemicals, no recharging.

### WarnFeet (medical, in development)

A **battery-free connected insole that helps prevent diabetic foot ulcers.** It converts the energy of each step into electricity (piezoelectric sensors) and monitors pressure and temperature under the foot in real time across **150 measurement zones** — powered solely by walking, with no battery to charge or replace. First prototype presented at **CES 2024**. **Status:** pre-commercial; clinical trials planned. **Price:** TBD (emerging). **ICP:** people living with diabetes at risk of foot ulceration, and the clinicians, podiatrists, and health systems who care for them. **Why it matters:** diabetic foot ulcers are a leading cause of lower-limb amputation; continuous, effortless monitoring catches the early pressure-and-temperature signals that precede a wound.

## Markets & ICPs

Solecooler serves two distinct markets off one technology platform.

### 1. Comfort & performance (ClimFeet) — consumer and B2B

**Personas & pains:**
- **Cold-feet professionals** — refrigerated-warehouse staff, construction and outdoor trades, ski-lift and winter workers. Pain: standing or moving for hours in the cold; existing heated insoles need charging mid-shift and only heat.
- **Athletes & outdoor enthusiasts** — runners, hikers, hunters, winter-sports users. Pain: temperature swings within one outing; bulky electronics; wanting cooling in summer, not just heat in winter.
- **First responders & military** — firefighters, soldiers. Pain: extreme, unpredictable conditions; reliability matters; batteries are a liability.
- **Everyday cold/hot feet & circulation issues** — people who are simply uncomfortable, or who have poor circulation (ClimFeet+). Pain: nothing on the market both warms *and* cools without electronics.

**Shared dream:** put the insole in, forget about it, and have comfortable feet all day in any season — nothing to charge, nothing to refill.

### 2. Medical prevention (WarnFeet)

**Personas & pains:** diabetic patients at risk of foot ulcers; podiatrists, diabetologists, and care teams; health systems and insurers paying for the downstream cost of amputations. Pain: ulcers develop silently; existing monitoring is intermittent or relies on devices that need charging. Dream: continuous, effortless, battery-free monitoring that flags risk early.

## Competitive Landscape

Solecooler's defensible edge is the same in every comparison: **it heats *and* cools, and it needs no battery and no chemicals.** No competitor does both.

| Category / competitor type | Their weakness | Solecooler's advantage |
|---|---|---|
| **Battery-powered heated insoles** (e.g. Therm-ic, Hotronic, ThermaCell, Sidas heat) | Heat only, never cool; need charging (hours of runtime, then dead); heavier with battery packs; electronics fail and wear out | Heats and cools, runs forever on footstep energy, nothing to charge, lighter, no electronics to fail |
| **Disposable chemical toe/foot warmers** (e.g. HotHands-style) | Single-use waste, heat only, fade after hours, recurring cost, not sustainable | Reusable for years, reversible heat/cool, zero consumable cost, recyclable and CO2-free in use |
| **Standard cushioning / orthotic insoles** | No thermoregulation at all | Adds heating and cooling on top of shock absorption |
| **Smart/connected insoles for health** (WarnFeet's space) | Depend on batteries that must be recharged; intermittent data | Battery-free continuous monitoring, powered by walking; 150 pressure/temperature zones |
