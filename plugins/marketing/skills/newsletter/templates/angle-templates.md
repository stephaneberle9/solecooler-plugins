# Newsletter Templates by Angle

Each template maps to one of the 10 Angles of Attraction. Every template uses either a **Snapshot** (Time/Location/Sensory scene) or **Snipershot** (direct hook) opener.

---

## 1. Opinions Template

**Set the scene with strong belief**
- Snapshot: how you found it (Time/Location/Sensory)
- Snipershot: open with opinion

**Continue conversation**
- Tie opinion into problem — why should your reader care?

**Present solution**
- Explain what to do instead
- Explain benefits

**Ask for action**
- Pivot to your pitch

---

## 2. Observations Template

**Set the scene with observation**
- Snapshot: where you observed it (TLS)
- Snipershot: begin with observation

**Continue conversation**
- Tie observation to problem — why should your reader care?

**Present solution**
- Explain how to avoid or take advantage of observation
- Explain benefits

**Ask for action**
- Pivot to your pitch

---

## 3a. Prediction (Negative) Template

**Set the scene with prediction**
- Snapshot: what prompted you to make it? (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with prediction

**Paint picture of the future**
- Tie prediction to problem — why should your reader care?

**Present solution**
- Give new path: "what to do instead"
- Explain benefits

**Ask for action**
- Present pitch as vehicle

## 3b. Prediction (Positive) Template

**Set the scene with prediction**
- Snapshot: what prompted you to make it? (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with prediction

**Paint picture of the future**
- Tie into your reader's desires

**Present the problem**
- Explain what's stopping them from this future

**Ask for action**
- Present pitch as vehicle

## 4. Obstacles Template

**Set the scene with obstacle**
- Snapshot: how you found it (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with obstacle

**Continue conversation**
- Tie obstacle into problem — why should your reader care?

**Present solution**
- Show them why the obstacle is overcomeable or not real
- Explain benefits of overcoming

**Ask for action**
- Pitch is the vehicle

---

## 5. Personal Storytelling Template

**Set the scene at the height of the action**
- Snapshot: describe the scene (TLS)
- Snipershot: an intriguing introduction

**Provide context**
- Once hooked, describe situation
- Explain what's at stake
- If possible, tie to reader's problem

**Continue story**
- Use "But and Therefore" to tell and finish the story
- Make sure all loops are closed

**Explain lesson**
- Why should your reader care? (bridge lesson)

**Ask for action**
- Tie pitch to point

---

## 6. Client Story Template

**Set the scene by introducing client**
- Snapshot: describe how you met client and their situation (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with client's problem

**Describe hell**
- How client was struggling (specific)
- What actions they had taken (especially trying other solutions)

**Pivot to heaven**
- Where they are now after working with you (specific)

**Explain how they got there**
- What, not how.

**Ask for action**
- Your offer is the bridge

(Can also go: hell → what → heaven)

## 8. Audience Question Template

**Set the scene with audience question**
- Snapshot: describe how/when the question came in (TLS)

**Continue conversation**

**Present solution**

**Ask for action**

- Snapshot: how you found it (TLS)

**Continue conversation**

**Present your view**

## 10. Current Trends Template

**Set the scene with event**
- Snapshot: describe what happened (TLS)
- Snipershot: dive right in

**Continue conversation**
- Explain further if required
- Tie into problem/your niche (if not already)

**Present solution**
- Why is this event important?
- Explain benefits of your point

**Ask for action**
- Tie pitch into event

## Bonus: The Listicle Template

**Set the scene with problem**
- Snapshot: how you found the problem (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with problem

**Present listicle**
- +/- explanations of points
- Usually odd number of points

**Ask for action**
- Tie listicle to purpose
- Pitch your offer as the HOW

---

## Bonus: Opening an Offer Template

**Set the scene by opening with the problem or promise**
- Snapshot: what have you been up to? (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with problem/promise

**Create tension**
- If opened with problem, describe heaven
- If opened with promise, describe hell

**Ask for action**
- Present your product with excitement (benefits)
- Include scarcity and urgency
- Include proof (especially if no sales page)

**P.S.**
- Ask for questions
- Gentle reminder re deadline

---

## Bonus: Closing an Offer Template

**Set the scene with reminder**
- Snapshot: indirect angle (TLS)
- Snipershot: dive into deadline

**Give reason to buy now**
- Restate the primary promise
- Explain deadline is xyz
- Explain cost of inaction

**Ask for action**
- Present pitch
- Remind urgency and scarcity

**P.S.**
- Answer objections (optional)
- Include proof (optional)
- Explain deadline final (timer?)

---

## Bonus: Self-Selection Conversation Starter Template

**Set the scene with polarization**
- Snapshot: explain why writing about this (TLS)
- Snipershot: open with the types

**Ask for action**
- Which does your reader relate to?

**P.S.**
- Let them know your views (influence direction)

---

## Bonus: The Waitlist Template

**Set the scene with thanks**
- Be energetic and interesting
- Tie it into the core promise of the offer

**Let them know about launch dates**
- If unsure, give an estimate

**Ask for action (questionnaire)**
- Frame as benefit (to help you nail it for them)
