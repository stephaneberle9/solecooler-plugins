# Channel Guide: Detailed Reference

Deep patterns, hook archetypes, and field-tested structures for every channel. Use this to understand what actually moves the needle.

## LinkedIn: The Full Picture

### How the Algorithm Works (December 2025)

LinkedIn runs a three-stage pipeline: quality filtering (spam, low, or high classification), engagement testing (your post gets shown to a small initial audience in the first hour), and relevance ranking (matching content to user interests and poster expertise).

**Signal priority stack:**

| Rank | Signal | How It Works |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Dwell time | Seconds spent reading. Still the dominant factor. |
| 2 | Topic authority | Posting consistently in one niche builds trust with the algorithm over time. |
| 3 | Authentic engagement | AI flags fake engagement by analyzing comment speed, patterns, and language. |
| 4 | Relevance over recency | Mid-2025 update resurfaces posts up to 2-3 weeks old if they match a user's interests. |
| 5 | Native content | Posts without outbound links get meaningfully more distribution. |

**The golden hour:** What happens in the first 60 minutes decides whether your post breaks out to 2nd and 3rd-degree connections. Strong early engagement triggers exponential reach.

**Where things stand now:** Organic views are down roughly 50%, engagement down roughly 25%, and follower growth down roughly 59%. The platform is prioritizing quality, relevance, and genuine expertise over posting volume.

### Slide Deck Mechanics

**Why slide decks win on LinkedIn:**
Every swipe adds dwell time. Educational content outperforms everything else here. Save-worthy posts get algorithmic gold. And slide decks can be adapted for Instagram with minimal changes.

**Optimal slide-by-slide structure:**

| Slide | Job | Design Guidance |
|-------|-----|-----------------|
| 1 | Stop the scroll | Bold text, stripped-down design, builds curiosity |
| 2 | Deepen the hook | Explain why this matters or what most people get wrong |
| 3-7 | Deliver the goods | One point per slide, readable at thumbnail size |
| 8 | Quick recap | Summary slide for fast reference |
| 9 | Convert | Save, follow, repost prompts |

**Production specs:** 1080x1350px (4:5 ratio) for maximum feed real estate. Cap at 10 slides. Body text minimum 24pt, headlines 40pt or larger. High contrast (dark on light or vice versa). Keep the design system consistent across all slides.

### Hook Archetypes for LinkedIn

These are the five structural patterns behind every high-performing LinkedIn opener. Each works differently, but they all create forward momentum.

**Archetype 1: The Hindsight Hook**
Structure: Personal failure or mistake, followed by the lesson.
> "I got fired in 2019. Best thing that ever happened. Here's why:"

Why it works: Vulnerability disarms skepticism. People lean in when someone admits a mistake because they want to know what changed.

**Archetype 2: The Contrarian Proof Hook**
Structure: Claim you stopped doing something popular, share results, then explain what replaced it.
> "I stopped posting daily on LinkedIn. My reach tripled. Here's what I do instead:"

Why it works: Goes against the grain of standard advice. The proof (results) gives permission to keep reading.

**Archetype 3: The Authority Reveal Hook**
Structure: Impressive credential or volume of experience, then a pattern you noticed.
> "After reviewing 500+ profiles this year, the same 5 mistakes keep showing up:"

Why it works: The number establishes credibility. The word "mistakes" triggers loss aversion.

**Archetype 4: The Prediction Hook**
Structure: Bold claim about what's coming, framed with urgency.
> "[Industry] is about to change forever. Here's what's coming:"

Why it works: People fear being left behind. Forward-looking content gets saved because readers want to reference it later.

**Archetype 5: The Puzzle Hook**
Structure: Ask why a surprising pattern exists, then promise the answer.
> "Why do some marketers grow fast while others plateau? I finally cracked it:"

Why it works: Questions engage the brain differently than statements. The promise of an answer creates a loop that needs closing.

### High-Performing Hook Examples

These structures have driven 1M+ impressions across multiple accounts:

- "I spent 10 years building the wrong career. Here's what I wish I knew at 25:"
- "The lie about 'working hard' that nobody talks about:"
- "Unpopular opinion: Your morning routine doesn't matter. Here's what does:"
- "I made $0 for 2 years. Then everything shifted. Here's what changed:"

## Twitter/X: The Full Picture

### How the Algorithm Works (December 2025)

X ranks the For You feed based on recency, engagement, relevance, media presence, account credibility, and content diversity.

**Signal priority stack:**

- **Replies** carry the most weight. Replies from accounts you regularly interact with count even more.
- **Quote tweets** deliver roughly double the engagement value of a standard retweet.
- **Time spent** on the tweet itself, plus time spent on any linked content.
- **Media boost** from images, videos, and GIFs that increase visibility scores.
- **Profile visits** triggered by the tweet signal curiosity and quality.

**Grok AI ranking:** The Following feed is no longer chronological. It uses Grok to rank posts based on your past interactions and topic interests. Users default to For You, not Following. Early engagement in the first hours is critical for amplification.

**Thread mechanics:** The first tweet determines roughly 90% of a thread's success. Sweet spot is 8-15 tweets. Every tweet needs to add value (no filler). Adding media to individual tweets boosts engagement. First-hour performance decides everything.

### Thread Architecture

**The Five-Part Structure:**

1. **The hook tweet** opens with a bold claim or promise, adds a one-line elaboration, and signals it's a thread.
2. **The setup tweet** gives brief context on why this matters and who needs to hear it.
3. **The body tweets** each contain a numbered point with a clear headline, 2-3 lines of explanation, and an example or proof point where possible.
4. **The summary tweet** delivers a TL;DR with bullet points recapping each main point.
5. **The close tweet** includes follow and retweet prompts plus a link if relevant.

### Hook Archetypes for X

**Archetype 1: The Eulogy**
Structure: Declare something dead and promise the replacement.
> "[Thing] is dead. Here's what's replacing it:"

Why it works: Finality gets attention. People need to know if they're investing in something obsolete.

**Archetype 2: The Experiment Report**
Structure: Describe what you did, for how long, and tease results.
> "I [did X] for [time period]. Here's what happened:"

Why it works: Real experience beats theory. The time investment signals commitment, and the open loop demands resolution.

**Archetype 3: The Grenade**
Structure: Acknowledge a group will be upset, then state the claim anyway.
> "This will piss off [group], but [claim]."

Why it works: Conflict is inherently attention-grabbing. Naming the opposition makes it specific.

**Archetype 4: The Vault**
Structure: Frame information as hidden or suppressed.
> "The [industry] secret no one talks about:"

Why it works: Scarcity of information triggers curiosity. "No one talks about" implies insider knowledge.

**Archetype 5: The Blueprint**
Structure: Specific result, specific timeframe, removal of common excuse, then the method.
> "[Specific result] in [timeframe]. No [common excuse]. Here's the playbook:"

Why it works: Specificity builds trust. Removing the excuse preemptively handles the reader's objection.

### High-Performing Tweet Examples

**Standalone tweets that drove 10K+ engagement:**
- "The best marketing doesn't feel like marketing."
- "Stop trying to go viral. Start trying to be useful."
- "Your competition isn't other companies. It's obscurity."
- "Most 'overnight successes' took 5+ years. They just didn't document year 1-4."

**Thread openers that drove massive engagement:**
- "I've spent $50M on ads. Here's what I'd do differently:"
- "10 businesses you can start with $0:"
- "The psychology behind viral content (based on 500+ examples):"

## Instagram: The Full Picture

### How the Algorithm Works (December 2025)

Instagram uses separate ranking systems for Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore. Each weighs signals differently.

**Signal priority stack:**

| Rank | Signal | How It Works |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | DM shares ("sends per reach") | Strongest discovery signal, especially for Reels and Explore |
| 2 | Saves | Indicates lasting value. Critical for Feed and Explore |
| 3 | Watch time and completion | For Reels, completion rate dominates |
| 4 | Likes per reach | Quality signal based on ratio, not raw count |
| 5 | Relationship signals | DMs, profile taps, and comment history strengthen your visibility to those users |
| 6 | Early velocity | Performance in the first 30-90 minutes decides wider distribution |

**Current state:** Photos are getting renewed support in the Feed (per Adam Mosseri). Slide decks expanded to 20 slides in some regions. Hashtag weighting dropped significantly. New "Your Algorithm" feature shows users why they see what they see.

**Reels vs. slide decks:** Reels are the best discovery tool (7-15 seconds for viral, 30-45 for tutorials). Slide decks drive the highest engagement and saves (6-10 slide sweet spot). Photos are getting renewed support, especially for photo-native audiences.

### Slide Deck Deep Dive

**Why slide decks dominate Instagram:**
Each swipe adds time-on-post, which signals the algorithm. Swipe-through rate separately signals interest. The format is inherently save-worthy for educational content. And you can tell a story across slides in a way no other format allows.

**Slide-by-slide breakdown:**

| Slide | Goal | Design Principle |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Stop the scroll | Bold text, curiosity, minimal visual clutter |
| 2 | Pull them deeper | "Here's the problem" or "Most people think..." |
| 3-7 | Deliver value | One point per slide, readable, visual hierarchy |
| 8-9 | Quick reference | Summary for people who swiped through fast |
| 10 | Convert | Save, follow, share prompts |

### Reel Mechanics

**The 3-second rule:** 65% of viewers decide to keep watching or scroll away within the first 3 seconds. Front-load the hook both visually and verbally. Start mid-action, never with an intro.

**Length benchmarks:**
- 7-15 seconds: Highest completion rate
- 15-30 seconds: Strong for tutorials
- 30-60 seconds: Works if value is dense
- 60-90 seconds: Only for highly engaged audiences

**Five Reel structures that perform:**

1. **Talking head plus text overlay.** You speaking with key points as text on screen. Works best for thought leadership.
2. **Before/After.** Show the transformation upfront. Works best for results-based content.
3. **POV plus scenario.** "POV: You're [relatable situation]." Works best for humor and relatability.
4. **Tutorial/How-to.** Walk through the process on screen. Works best for actionable content.
5. **Trending audio with a niche twist.** Use a trending sound and apply your industry's angle. Works best for discovery.

### Caption Structures

**The Narrative Caption:**
Open with a hook line that works in the preview. Tell a personal story with a setup, a struggle, and a turning point. Close with the lesson and a question for the audience. Finish with save/share/comment prompts and 3 hashtags.

**The Rundown Caption:**
Open with a hook that combines a number and a benefit. List 3-5 numbered points with brief explanations. Close with "Which one resonates most?"

**The Lesson Caption:**
Open with a contrarian or surprising statement. Explain what most people miss in 2-3 paragraphs. Offer 3 alternative actions to try. Close with "Save this for later."

## TikTok: The Full Picture

### How the Algorithm Works (December 2025)

TikTok's 2025 algorithm uses enhanced personalization, weighing first-second retention, niche community signals, contextual content classification, and production quality.

**Signal priority stack:**
- **First-second retention** is everything. Completion rate is number one, but the opening seconds carry disproportionate weight.
- **Rewatch rate** from multiple views is a powerful boost.
- **DM shares** rank higher than public shares.
- **Niche community alignment** now beats broad virality. Specialized audiences get rewarded.
- **Contextual categorization** lets the AI distinguish humor from education from emotion, matching content to the right interests.
- **Production quality** is now a ranking factor. Lighting, sound, and editing all count.

**How distribution works:** TikTok shows your video to small initial groups (roughly 200-500 people). If engagement is strong, it goes to the next batch. Each batch has to perform for the next one to see it. This is why the first 3 seconds make or break everything.

**Current state:** The algorithm now rewards longer content (30-60+ seconds) when retention stays high. Niche communities (#BookTok, #MarketingTok) get boosted over generic viral content. Deeper AI personalization analyzes watch duration, replays, and cross-platform habits.

### Hook Mastery

**The 3-second audit:** For every hook, answer three questions. What is the viewer seeing visually? What are they hearing? What question or curiosity is being planted?

**Five Hook Archetypes:**

**Archetype 1: The Identity Flag**
Call out a specific group so they self-select.
> "This is for my [specific group]..."
> "If you're a [identity], you need to hear this"

Why it works: People stop scrolling when they feel personally addressed. Specificity beats broadness every time.

**Archetype 2: The Interrupt**
Break the scroll pattern with something unexpected.
> Start mid-action with no intro. Unexpected visual that doesn't match the audio. Jump cut straight to the key moment.

Why it works: The brain flags anything that doesn't fit the expected pattern. Novelty triggers attention.

**Archetype 3: The Firestarter**
Lead with something you know will provoke a reaction.
> "I'm gonna get hate for this but..."
> "[Popular opinion] is wrong. Here's why."

Why it works: Disagreement is engagement. People watch to either validate their own position or see if you change their mind.

**Archetype 4: The Result Reveal**
Show the outcome first, then explain the path.
> Show the end result immediately, then walk backward through how you got there.

Why it works: The payoff up front earns permission to explain the process. People are more patient when they know the destination is worth it.

**Archetype 5: The Knowledge Drop**
Frame your content as information someone is trying to keep from the viewer.
> "The thing about [topic] that nobody talks about..."
> "I can't believe [brand/industry] doesn't want you to know this"

Why it works: Perceived secrecy creates urgency. The viewer feels like they're getting access to something exclusive.

### Video Structure Patterns

**Quick Teach (15-30 seconds):**
0-3 seconds is the bold claim hook. 3-25 seconds covers 3-5 rapid points. 25-30 seconds is the call to action or loop point.

**Story Arc (30-60 seconds):**
0-3 seconds starts in the middle ("So there I was..."). 3-10 seconds sets up context. 10-45 seconds builds the story with tension. 45-60 seconds delivers the payoff and lesson.

**Myth Buster (15-45 seconds):**
0-3 seconds calls out the myth ("Stop believing [myth]"). 3-30 seconds explains why it's wrong and what's actually true. 30-45 seconds provides what to do instead.

**POV Play (15-30 seconds):**
0-3 seconds sets the scenario ("POV: [relatable scenario]"). 3-25 seconds acts it out. 25-30 seconds delivers the punchline or insight.

### TikTok-Specific Production Notes

1. **Sound selection:** Use trending sounds when relevant, even at low volume under voiceover.
2. **Captions are mandatory.** Over 80% of viewers watch without sound.
3. **Loop potential:** Ending where you started drives rewatches.
4. **Comment fuel:** End with a question or controversial statement that begs a reply.
5. **Series format:** "Part 1," "Episode 2" structuring drives follows.
6. **Green screen:** You in frame with supporting content (tweets, data, articles) behind you.

## YouTube: The Full Picture

### How the Algorithm Works (December 2025)

YouTube optimizes for viewer satisfaction and retention across both Shorts and long-form, using AI-driven personalization with stronger quality and authority checks.

**Video-level signals:**
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** How often people click when your video appears in their feed.
- **Average view duration plus percent watched:** Both raw watch time and completion percentage matter.
- **Session impact:** Whether your video keeps people on YouTube longer.
- **Viewer satisfaction:** YouTube uses surveys combined with behavioral signals to estimate quality.
- **Negative feedback:** "Not interested" clicks, skips, and very low retention actively hurt your ranking.

**Channel-level signals:**
- **Topical consistency:** Focused channels get recommended more than scattered ones.
- **Satisfaction history:** Based on surveys and engagement patterns over time.
- **Upload regularity and library depth:** More data gives the system more to work with.

**Current state:** AI-driven hyper-personalization factors in device type, time of day, and individual habits. Old evergreen videos get resurfaced when their topics trend again. Authority and depth are rewarded, aligned with Google Search updates. Shorts and long-form both feed the algorithm's understanding of your channel.

### Shorts vs. Long-Form Strategy

**YouTube Shorts:** Function as a discovery engine (similar to TikTok's For You page). Drive subscribers but generate lower watch-time value. Can adapt TikTok content. 10-35 seconds is optimal for discovery, up to 60 for stories.

**Long-form:** Functions as the monetization engine through ad revenue. Gets discovered through search and browse (evergreen). 8-12 minute sweet spot for most niches. Builds deeper audience relationships.

### Thumbnail Psychology

**The three-element rule:** Maximum 3 visual elements on any thumbnail. Face, text, and object (or any combination of 2-3).

**What drives clicks:**

| Element | Why It Pulls |
|---------|-------------|
| Expressive face | Emotion is magnetic at any size |
| Bold text (3-4 words max) | Adds context that works at thumbnail scale |
| Before/after | Curiosity about the transformation |
| Contrast colors | Stands out in a crowded feed |
| Curiosity gap | Shows the outcome, not the process |

**What kills clicks:** Cluttered design, generic images, too much text, low contrast, neutral facial expression.

**CTR benchmarks (December 2025):**
- Home and Suggested: 4-10% is typical, 6-12% is strong for established channels
- Search-driven content: 8-15% for well-targeted tutorials and how-to topics
- Shorts: CTR is less relevant. Swipe-through vs. swipe-away is the real metric.

Note: YouTube balances CTR against watch time and satisfaction. Clickbait that gets clicks but poor retention gets punished.

### Title Structures That Work

**The "How" family:**
- "How I [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe]"
- "How to [Achieve Outcome] (Step-by-Step)"
- "How [Known Person/Company] [Does Thing]"

**The number family:**
- "[Number] [Things] That [Outcome]"
- "[Number] Mistakes [Role] Make"
- "[Number] Signs [Situation]"

**The curiosity family:**
- "Why [Surprising Thing] Is [Counterintuitive Truth]"
- "The [Adjective] [Thing] That [Outcome]"
- "What [Expert/Company] Doesn't Want You to Know About [Topic]"

**The transformation family:**
- "I [Did X] for [Time Period]. Here's What Happened."
- "From [State A] to [State B]: My Journey"
- "[Result] in [Timeframe]: Exactly How I Did It"

### Long-Form Structure: The AIDA Approach

**Attention (0-30 seconds):** Pattern interrupt or bold claim. Quick credibility if needed. Preview of what's coming. "Stay until the end for [bonus]."

**Interest (30 seconds to 2 minutes):** Why this matters. Who this is for. What you'll cover. Build anticipation.

**Desire (main body):** Deliver on the promise. Clear sections with verbal signposting ("First... Next... Finally..."). Examples and proof for every point.

**Action (final 30 seconds):** Summarize key points. Give a clear next step. End screen with subscribe button and next video recommendation.

### Shorts Patterns

**The Speed Tip:** Hook with "[Number]-second [topic] tip." Deliver immediately. Close with "Subscribe for more."

**The Clip:** Best moment from a long-form video, self-contained so it works alone. Close with "Full video on my channel."

**The Transformation:** Show the result first. Quick explanation of how. Close with "Want the full tutorial?"

**The Reaction:** React to trending content in your niche. Share your take. Close with "What do you think?"

## The Distribution Calendar

### The 50-30-20 Split

Structure your weekly output in three tiers:

**50% value content** (roughly 5 posts per week): Slide decks, threads, educational Reels. This is your repurposed source material.

**30% engagement content** (roughly 3 posts per week): Questions, polls, spicy takes. These drive comments and conversation.

**20% personal content** (roughly 2 posts per week): Stories, behind-the-scenes, personal observations. These build the human connection.

## Channel-Specific Pitfalls

### LinkedIn
1. Putting links in the post body (kills reach; use comments instead)
2. Overloading hashtags (3-5 max)
3. Walls of text with no line breaks
4. Hashtags on slide deck slides
5. Writing with Twitter-level casualness (too informal for the platform)

### X
1. Threads longer than 20 tweets (fatigue sets in)
2. Weak first tweet (it decides everything)
3. Filler tweets that add no value
4. Ignoring replies (kills the engagement loop)
5. Posting at low-traffic hours

### Instagram
1. Only posting static images (algorithm penalizes it over time)
2. Hashtags in the wrong location
3. Video without captions
4. Reposting TikToks with the watermark visible
5. Inconsistent visual style across posts

### TikTok
1. Slow starts with no hook for 3+ seconds
2. Missing captions
3. Ignoring trending sounds entirely
4. Over-polished production (authenticity outperforms polish)
5. Not engaging with comments

### YouTube
1. Clickbait thumbnails that don't match the content
2. No hook in the first 30 seconds
3. Cluttered thumbnail design
4. Title that promises something the video doesn't deliver
5. Missing cards and end screens
