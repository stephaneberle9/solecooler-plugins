# Worked Examples

Three end-to-end examples showing how a single source piece splits into multi-channel content. Use as shape references when planning your own repurpose runs.

---

## Example 1 — Newsletter → multi-channel

**Source:** 2,000-word newsletter titled "5 Pricing Traps That Stall Online Course Sales"

**Central takeaway:** The way you price a course often matters more than the course itself.

**Building blocks:**
1. Anchoring with decoy tiers
2. Hiding the price below the fold
3. Over-discounting early
4. Using round numbers instead of psychologically-priced ones
5. Forgetting to justify the price against perceived value

### Output plan

| Channel | Format | What it covers |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| LinkedIn | Slide deck (8 slides) | Hook + 5 traps + recap + close |
| LinkedIn | Written post (1,400 chars) | Deep dive on trap #1 with a personal story |
| X | Thread (7 tweets) | Hook + 5 traps (1 per tweet) + wrap |
| X | Standalone tweet | Just trap #3 (most counterintuitive) as a spicy take |
| Instagram | Slide deck (8 slides) | Visual version of the LinkedIn deck |
| Instagram | Reel (30 sec) | "Stop making these pricing mistakes" |
| TikTok | Video (20 sec) | Most controversial trap, hot take delivery |
| YouTube Short | Video (45 sec) | All 5 traps, rapid fire |

**Total yield:** 8 channel pieces from one newsletter.

## Example 2 — Podcast interview → multi-channel

**Source:** 45-minute interview with a fitness coach about building an online business

**Central takeaway:** The fitness coaches who scale aren't the ones with the best workouts — they're the ones who understand positioning.

**Building blocks pulled from the interview:**
1. Guest quote about their first $10k month breakthrough
2. Framework: the "Problem-Promise-Proof" positioning structure
3. 10 best one-liners from the conversation
4. One viscerally good 30-second clip
5. The spiciest take (why most coaches fail at pricing)

### Output plan

| Channel | Format | What it covers |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| LinkedIn | Written post | Best guest quote + context + your take |
| LinkedIn | Slide deck | The Problem-Promise-Proof framework |
| X | Thread (10 tweets) | 10 best insights from the episode |
| X | Standalone tweet | Best quote as a standalone insight |
| Instagram | Slide deck | Visual quotes from the guest |
| Instagram | Reel (30 sec) | Best 30-second clip with captions |
| TikTok | Video | Spiciest take from the interview |
| YouTube Short | Video | Best insight with visual hook |
| YouTube | Long-form | Full episode or highlights compilation |

**Total yield:** 9 channel pieces from one interview.

## Example 3 — Landing page / sales page → multi-channel

**Source:** A live sales page for an 8-week SaaS onboarding course

**Central takeaway:** Most SaaS teams overthink their onboarding rebuild and underthink the 15 minutes after signup.

**Building blocks:**
1. The 3-pillar activation flow (first-run screens, activation emails, pricing page audit)
2. Case study: a client went 4.5% → 14% trial-to-paid in 60 days
3. 5 common onboarding mistakes (from the page's "What we fix" section)
4. The "don't fabricate proof" philosophy (from the methodology)
5. One standout testimonial

### Output plan

| Channel | Format | What it covers |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| LinkedIn | Slide deck (10 slides) | The 3-pillar framework, visualized |
| LinkedIn | Written post | Case study deep dive (4.5% → 14%) |
| X | Thread (12 tweets) | The full 3-pillar framework with mini-examples |
| X | Standalone tweet | The testimonial, framed as a hot take |
| X | Standalone tweet | The "don't fabricate proof" philosophy |
| Instagram | Slide deck (8 slides) | Visual version of the LinkedIn deck |
| Instagram | Reel (30 sec) | "Most SaaS onboarding is broken. Here's the fix." |
| TikTok | Video (45 sec) | The 5 mistakes, rapid fire |
| YouTube Short | Video (60 sec) | Case study outcome + how it was done |

**Total yield:** 9 pieces, with the thread + LP framework driving traffic back to the actual sales page.

## How to use these examples

1. **Don't copy the yield count.** Some sources are naturally richer than others. A dense blog post might yield 12; a short podcast clip might yield 4. Quality over quantity.
2. **Watch the central takeaway.** In every example it's one sentence — short enough to fit in a tweet. If yours is a paragraph, you haven't found it yet.
3. **Notice what's in / out per channel.** Each example skips channels that don't fit the source. Don't generate a TikTok if the source has zero visual energy.
4. **Watch the voice calibration.** Same building block, different delivery per channel — but the underlying voice stays consistent.
