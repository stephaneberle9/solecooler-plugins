# Worked Example: Online Cooking School

This is an alternate full worked example for the keyword-research skill. The primary worked example in SKILL.md uses a Claude Code workshop. This file preserves the cooking school walkthrough so the same Mine → Expand → Cluster → Score → Map process is illustrated against a different vertical.

---

## context gathered
- **Business:** Online cooking school with an 8-week home chef program
- **Audience:** Busy professionals 25-40 who want to stop relying on takeout and cook confidently at home
- **Goal:** Leads for the cooking program
- **Timeline:** Blend of fast wins and long-term authority

## starter keywords mined
- online cooking classes
- meal prep course
- quick weeknight dinners
- learn to cook for beginners
- healthy cooking at home
- home chef program

## expanded via prism scan (sample)

**Angle 1 (Your offer):** online cooking classes, 8-week chef program, virtual cooking lessons, knife skills course

**Angle 2 (Their frustration):** everything tastes bland, scared to try new recipes, keep burning food, wasting money on delivery apps

**Angle 3 (Desired result):** cook restaurant meals at home, healthy dinners in 20 minutes, host a dinner party confidently, impress a date with homemade food

**Angle 4 (Your edge):** cooking for absolute beginners, 30-minute meals only, chef who couldn't boil water at 25

**Angle 5 (Adjacent):** kitchen organization tips, grocery budgeting, food photography basics, wine pairing for beginners

**Angle 6 (Names/brands):** Blue Apron alternative, Gordon Ramsay techniques, MasterClass cooking review, cast iron cooking methods

## organized into anchors

**Anchor 1: Quick Weeknight Dinners** (Priority: Critical)
- 30-minute dinner recipes for busy people
- Weeknight meals with 5 ingredients or less
- Meal prep vs cooking fresh: which saves more time
- Common cooking mistakes that slow you down

**Anchor 2: Cooking for Complete Beginners** (Priority: Critical)
- How to start cooking with zero experience
- Kitchen tools every beginner actually needs
- Simple recipes that teach real technique
- Overcoming fear of the kitchen

**Anchor 3: Online Cooking Classes** (Priority: High)
- Are online cooking classes worth the money
- Online classes vs in-person cooking school
- How to choose an online cooking program
- What to expect from a virtual cooking course

## top 3 recommendations

**1. "Quick Weeknight Dinners for Busy Professionals" (First Priority)**
- Perfect audience match
- Competition is generic "easy recipes" content, not targeted to professionals
- Practitioner credibility: real student results and tested recipes
- Flagship guide, 5,000+ words

**2. "How to Start Cooking When You Have Zero Experience" (Second Priority)**
- Large search volume across the cluster
- Current top results are surface-level listicles
- Unique angle: transformation stories from students who went from takeout-only to confident cooks
- Comprehensive guide, 3,000+ words

**3. "Are Online Cooking Classes Worth It?" (Quick Win)**
- Commercial intent, searchers are close to buying
- Thin competitor content in this space
- Natural fit for weaving in student testimonials and course details
- Comparison piece, 2,500+ words
