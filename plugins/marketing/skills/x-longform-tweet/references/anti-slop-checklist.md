# Anti-Slop Checklist for Tweets

Quick-scan checklist. Run through this before posting anything.

## Structural Tells

| AI Pattern | What to Do Instead |
|---|---|
| "It's not about X, it's about Y" | Just say what it IS about |
| Three adjectives in a row | Use one. Or two. Or four. |
| "The catch?" / "The kicker?" / "Here's the thing" | Delete it. Start the next line. |
| -ing verb as sentence opener ("Building an audience...") | Flip it: "I build..." or "It takes time to build..." |
| Perfect paragraph balance (3 lines, 3 lines, 3 lines) | Vary: 1 line, then 4, then 2 |
| Em dash chains (thought — and another — and another) | One em dash per post max. Use periods. |
| Semicolons | Never in tweets. Period. |

## Word-Level Tells

### Banned Words (instant AI detection)
utilize, leverage, facilitate, optimize, implement, synergy, game-changer, revolutionary, supercharge, transform, unlock, actionable, unpack, deep dive, circle back, move the needle, low-hanging fruit, ecosystem (unless literally ecology), landscape (unless literally geography), robust, seamless, cutting-edge, paradigm, streamline, holistic

### Banned Phrases
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "It's important to understand"
- "Let that sink in"
- "Read that again"
- "This is the way"
- "No fluff. No [X]. Just [Y]."
- "If you're serious about..."
- "To your success"
- "Not because of X. But because of Y."
- "Enter: [thing]"
- "Yesterday, someone said something that stopped me in my tracks"
- "Here's what nobody tells you about"
- "I spent X hours researching Y so you don't have to"

### Hedge Words (delete on sight)
- "It's worth considering"
- "You might want to think about"
- "Perhaps"
- "It could be argued that"
- "Interestingly enough"
- "Needless to say" (then don't say it)

## Rhythm Tells

AI writes in a steady, predictable rhythm. Human writing is jagged.

**AI rhythm:**
```
The market is shifting rapidly. Companies that adapt will thrive.
Those that don't will struggle. The key is to move fast and
iterate quickly. Success comes to those who embrace change.
```

**Human rhythm:**
```
the market is shifting.

most companies wont adapt. they'll add an "AI" badge to their
existing product and call it innovation. meanwhile some kid in
a studio apartment just built their replacement in a weekend.

thats the part nobody wants to talk about.
```

Differences:
- Uneven paragraph lengths
- One-line punches between longer sections
- Specific detail ("kid in a studio apartment") not abstract ("those who embrace change")
- Trailing thought at the end, not a tidy conclusion

## Voice Authenticity Check

Read your tweet and ask:
1. Would I say this to a friend at a bar? If no, rewrite.
2. Could I swap in any other topic? If yes, the opinion isn't specific enough.
3. Is every sentence doing work? If any sentence is "filler" padding, cut it.
4. Does the last line earn its spot? Or is it a reflex conclusion? Cut reflex conclusions.
5. Count -ing words. More than 2 in the whole tweet? Rewrite the worst ones.

## Platform-Specific Rules

Twitter is not LinkedIn. Twitter is not a blog. These matter:

- **No hashtags** (unless ironic or you're specifically told to)
- **No "1/12 🧵" thread openers** — this skill writes single posts, not threads
- **No emoji bullet points** (🔑 💡 🚀) — that's linkedin energy
- **Links go at the end** or in a reply, never mid-sentence
- **@mentions only for real people** you're referencing
- **Images/screenshots**: suggest them, don't describe them in text

## The Final Test

Before you paste it into twitter, read the first 3 words out loud.

If those 3 words don't make someone want to read word 4, rewrite the opening.

The scroll is merciless. You get 0.5 seconds.
