**Example (681k views, 7689 likes):**
```

1. You have 1-click n8n, Zapier, and Lindy AI agent templates to automate your content, outreach, research, reporting, anything.

6. You can summarize 300-page PDFs in seconds.
7. You can turn rough Looms into polished blog posts instantly.
8. You can generate personalized landing pages for every visitor.
9. You can talk to your data like it's ChatGPT.
10. You can train a custom GPT on your company docs and have it answer support tickets 24/7.
11. You can A/B test 100 ad creatives before you spend a dollar... all AI-generated.
12. You can scrape the internet, analyze competitors, and generate go-to-market strategies overnight.
13. You can deploy voice agents that call leads, qualify them, and update your CRM... in your voice.
14. TLDR; you can do a lot, my friend

```

**What makes it work:**
- "Okay, let me get this straight..." = conversational opener, not thesis
- 13 items (not the AI-default 5)
- each item is a specific capability, not vague
- "TLDR; you can do a lot, my friend" = casual break before close
- two-word close: "Enjoy it." — no elaboration needed

### G2: Step-by-Step Framework

**Example (422k views, 4904 likes):**
```
sam altman just dropped a blog post on why we're entering the era of the "idea guy"

so, i wrote down 19 ways to come up with startup ideas in the AI age:

1. ask chatgpt: "give me 10 tedious workflows a [job title] does that ai could automate." rinse and niche down.

2. scroll your own chatgpt history. if you're repeating the same prompt weekly, that's a product hiding in plain sight.

3. search: site:reddit.com "is there a tool that" + your niche. you'll find users begging for tools that don't exist.

4. audit where you copy/paste between tools. that friction is a startup idea.
...
```

**What makes it work:**
- hooks into current event (Sam Altman blog post)
- "so, i wrote down" = casual framing, not "I've compiled a comprehensive guide"
- each item is actionable and specific
- drops real platforms (reddit, chatgpt, fiverr, g2, linkedin)
- "rinse and niche down" = casual language in the instructions

## Signull Examples

### S1: Cultural Analysis

**Example (2.6M views, 18779 likes):**
```
zuck's playbook for poaching ai talent is absolutely savage.

openai is obviously not acquirable, at least not in any real world scenario. so what's the next best move? you don't buy the company. you bleed it out. you go straight for the people who are the company.

openai is quite literally nothing without its people, remember? especially in ai. so zuck starts cutting checks big enough to short circuit anyone's loyalty circuits. $100m, $300m+ packages.

it's just a different kind of acquisition. just one quiet defection at a time until the center can't hold. everyone's still stuck in traditional acquisition era thinking, while zuck's out here doing black ops recruitment like it's cold war berlin.
```

**What makes it work:**
- "absolutely savage" — strong judgment in opener, not hedged
- "you don't buy the company. you bleed it out." — short, punchy, violent verb
- "short circuit anyone's loyalty circuits" — playful phrase
- "cold war berlin" — unexpected analogy that elevates the analysis
- no numbered list, no bullet points — flowing prose

**Example (506k views, 4438 likes):**
```
this is a high tier psyop from sam. he's talking directly to the market. by floating a number like $100m:

- he sets an anchor so absurd that anything meta offers now feels cheap.
- he forces meta to either deny or match, both of which hurt them.
- he gives his own employees a loyalty premium, they feel like they're sitting on a $100m chip.
- he blurs price discovery for everyone else. no recruiter knows the real going rate anymore.
```

**What makes it work:**
- "high tier psyop" — strong framing, not "interesting strategic move"
- bullet list but each item explains a MECHANISM, not restates the thesis
- "& most importantly" — the ampersand is signull's signature
- close uses actual frameworks: "information asymmetry + narrative warfare"
- "by responding at all" — shows the deeper game

### S2: Paradox/Tension Reveal

**Example (590k views, 4011 likes):**
```
my favorite reaction to this is: "well let's just fix it."

lol. it's not fixable.

the problem isn't low birthrates, it's that the forces driving them are self reinforcing. economic uncertainty, cultural nihilism, hyperindividualism, & infinite digital dopamine loops. all spiraling inward.

even if you paid people $1m to have kids, most wouldn't. incentives don't rewire meaning. & once culture tips, it behaves like physics… entropy only increases.

birthrate collapse is a ratchet. once a generation internalizes childlessness not as crisis but virtue, it's over. there is no rewind button on belief.
```

**What makes it work:**
- opens by quoting the naive response, then demolishes it
- "lol." — one word, period. dismissive and effective.
- uses physics vocabulary (entropy, ratchet) without being academic
- "there is no rewind button on belief" — closing line hits hard because it's not trying to

### S3: Analogy Framework

**Example (63k views, 776 likes):**
```
when you're crafting content, you have to think in terms of half life, how long the content stays alive relative to the platform.

x is like glucose: fast spike, fast crash. viral or dead in 24h.

substack is more like protein: slower digesting, builds over time. dense, reusable.

youtube is fiber: lives forever in search, but digestion sometimes takes effort.

tiktok is caffeine: gets in fast, jacks your system.

instagram is aspartame: optimized for visual affect, not intellectual recall. sweet, addictive, & near zero nutrition.

this is an important framing to keep in mind. the platform norms define the half life.
```

**What makes it work:**
- single metaphor (nutrition/digestion) extended across 5 platforms
- each gets one line — tight, parallel
- "aspartame" for instagram is the standout — unexpected, slightly mean, memorable
- close is one sentence, not a paragraph

## Cross-Pattern Vocabulary Reference

### Words that signal human writing
specific numbers, tool names (n8n, claude, perplexity, stripe), casual profanity (shit, damn, wtf), abbreviations (tbh, imo, ngl), conversational fillers ("like," "kinda," "basically"), real company/person references

### Words that signal AI writing
leverage, utilize, optimize, facilitate, transform, revolutionary, game-changing, actionable insights, deep dive, robust, seamless, holistic, paradigm, synergy, landscape, ecosystem (in business context), unpack, circle back, double down (when not about investment), align on, at the end of the day
