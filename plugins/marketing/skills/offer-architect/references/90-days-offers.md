# 90 Days of Offers

The tactical playbook for daily selling.

---

## The KPI

If someone shows up at a hospital with a flu and a missing arm, which one would you treat first?

Likes, engagement, subs—those are the flu.
**Offers Made** is the missing arm.

Until we fix offer frequency, the rest doesn't matter.

---

## Where to Sell

**Primary channel: Email.**

People follow you because they want information.
People subscribe because they have a problem.
Email is more natural for selling.

### If No List
- Post emails on socials, change CTA to DM
- Make separate posts to get people to join list
- Gets you sales + email list growth

### If You Have a List
- Copy-paste emails to socials 1+ day after sending
- Change CTA to join list

---

## When to Sell

**Every day.**

If it's slow? Send two.

Will you get unsubscribers? Yes.
Will you make more money? Also yes.

When in doubt, make more offers.

---

## What to Sell

Don't need different offers. Sell different parts of your Offer Shell:

| Type | What It Is |
|------|------------|
| Core Offer | Your Google Doc Offer |
| Workshop | Training you'll do for clients anyway |
| Waitlist | If Core Offer sells too well or too bad |
| Level of Access | When others feel exhausted, vary it |
| Recycle | Successful workshops/courses (run multiple times) |
| Bundle | Workshops/modules that work together |

---

## How to Sell: Red Velvet

**Red velvet = chocolate cake with red coloring.**

Changing a small thing about your offer makes it look new.
People love NEW.

Never promote same offer more than 2 weeks in a row.
Taking things down sends message: "Things go away here."
This creates speed. Speed creates cash.

### Red Velvet Components

| Scarcity | Urgency |
|----------|---------|
| Limited spots (evergreen or current) | Price raise |
| 1-on-1 spots | Periodic price raise |
| Bonuses (add them) | Closing doors at date |
| Bonuses (take them away) | Seasonal urgency |
| DFY work included | Invent any excuse |
| Never available again | |

**Formula:** Varying Offers + Red Velvet + Daily Emails = $$$

## Email Types

### 90% Insight-Based

Value attracts people who want to TRY your stuff.
Insight attracts people who want to BUY your stuff.

**Value = giving the answer.**
**Insight = showing you have it.**

| Value-Based | Insight-Based |
|-------------|---------------|
| "Here's how to structure your offer: Price X at this, Y at that, use these platforms. Here's the template." | "The 41 Minute Workday" |

### 4 Insight Buckets

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Problem** | "My X pretty much sucks" / "This email is written by a client" |
| **Outcome** | "3 Clients. 3 Days. 3 Steps." / "I bought an apartment (cash)" / "I did very little" |
| **Idea** | "Whale Bait" / "90 Days Of Offers Swipe File" / "Content Creators vs. Cash Creators" |
| **Leadership** | "I'm Splitting Up With My Business Partner" / "What if scaling is the wrong move?" |

### 10% Sales Emails

**Hand Raisers:**
> "Would you like an early invitation?"

**Workshop Template:**
> "[Workshop Name] Workshop Invitation"

**Core Offer Template:**
> "Your Invitation Inside"

**Urgency Emails:**
- "Last Day: [Offer]"
- "6 Hours Left"
- "9 Hours Left"

## Recycling

People forget what they had for breakfast.
They forgot what email you sent them last month.

**Repurpose top emails every 6-8 weeks.**

When you find your top 10, make that your Welcome Sequence.

---

## The Daily Rhythm

### Every Day
- [ ] Make 1 Front End offer (email + social)
- [ ] Post Netflix-style content (updates, events, emotions)

### Every Week
- [ ] Make 1 Back End offer to current clients
- [ ] Review who missed out, start conversations

### Every 2 Weeks Max
- [ ] Red Velvet your offer (change something)
- [ ] Cycle to different offer type

### Every Month
- [ ] Open/close an intake
- [ ] Update Protagonist progress

### Every Quarter
- [ ] Raise prices at milestone
- [ ] Big Protagonist Update
- [ ] Launch a Minigame
