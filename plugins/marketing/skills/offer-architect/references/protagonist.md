# The Protagonist

Position yourself as a leader people follow, not just another coach.

---

## Coach vs Protagonist

| Coaches | Protagonists |
|---------|--------------|
| Exist in circles | Has a goal and actions |
| Creates | Updates |
| Indecisive | Decisive |
| Always there | There for now |
| Social media mindset | Netflix mindset |
| React to market | Happen to market |

**The "aura":** People who seem to have a special aura aren't first because of it. They have it because they're willing to go first.

---

## Why You Can't Lose

The "perceived risk" of being a Protagonist: If you fail, everyone sees it.

**Reality:**
- Rocky lost his first fight... We still watched Rocky 2
- Luke lost his hand and friend... We still watched Return of the Jedi
- Walter White became a murderer... People still rewatch it

**People fall in love with characters, not outcomes.**

If I get to $100k/month: "I did it. Here's the cohort showing how."
If I don't: "I failed to build $100k/month. I just built $92k/month. Anyway, here's the cohort."

Coaches can lose because they play someone else's game.
Protagonists can't because they make the rules.

## The Protagonist Post

Your pinned post for the entire year. Constantly quote, reference, update, and remind people of it.

### 3 Parts

#### 1. Your Goal
Must genuinely excite YOU. If it doesn't excite you, it won't excite them.

Options:
- Money: "My $100k/month profit"
- Impact: "Help 1000 people get a six pack"
- Capture: "Create $5M/month for clients, capture $50k"
- Build: "Proyecto Casa"

**Note:** The goal itself isn't that important. The bravery to declare it is.

#### 2. Your Steps
Lay out how you'll do things. This naturally promotes your Offer Shell.

Benefit: Eases resistance to offers all year because people were already told they'll be sold to. (You can't complain about being punched in a boxing gym.)

#### 3. Your Rules
What you're willing and NOT willing to do.

This makes people develop strong feelings about you (good thing).

**Example:** Batman doesn't kill. This is key to his character and makes us have stronger feelings.

**Why it works:** Promises attract low-income people. Philosophy attracts high-income people.

## Netflix Content Strategy

Treat social media like a TV show about you, not social media.

**Coach:** "I just hit a record month."
**Audience:** "Congrats."

**Protagonist:** "I just hit a record month. If we keep going at this rate we might hit the goal sooner than expected."
**Audience:** "What goal?"

### Big Updates

Plans will change. The path is formed as you walk it.

**Percentage Hits:**
- 10%, 20%, 25%, 33%, 50%, 60%, 70%, 75%, 80%, 85%, 90%, 95%, 100%

**Update Types:**
- Updates in business
- Updates in progress
- Plot twists (fired team, split with partner, pivoted)

You have to let go of insistence on certainty to write a great story.

### Small Updates

Most episodes don't move the plot forward. Doesn't matter—we watch for the Protagonist.

- Win
- Reminding of goal
- Event that day
- Event over the shoulder
- Physical movement
- Non-business related
- L's (but only AFTER W's)
- Statistics
- In hindsight
- Emotions
- Recycle (top ones every 6-8 weeks)

### Minigames

Games correlated to main goal but don't last long.

Examples:
- The Cash Creator Store
- The Baby Project
- The $100k In 30 Days Challenge

Minigames are exciting. Give people strong reason to come back.

## Conditioning Your Audience to Buy

Coaching is a luxury good sold as a commodity. People are used to coaches always being available and nice.

**Reality:** Millions need a problem solved. Only you solve it your way. YOU hold the status in the sale.

The Protagonist takes pieces away to condition buying.

### 5 Conditioning Tactics

#### 1. Strategic Price Raises
- At milestones: 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%
- Or every quarter
- Use as "reasons why" in marketing

#### 2. Private Consequences
> "I'm closing reservations tomorrow. Are we doing this or should I release yours?"

> "Have a read and let me know if you're in or have questions—I'm raising the price next quarter."

> "I'm closing your file because I didn't hear back from you."

#### 3. Start Convos with People Who Missed Out
> "You just missed this opening unfortunately. Would you like to see what reserving for the next one looks like?"

> "The replay is not for sale. What are you working on? I have other stuff that may be useful."

#### 4. Public Consequences
- Married Girl Strategy
- Reminders of GLOBAL and LOCAL caps
- "Doors closing" posts

#### 5. Take Things Away
- Bonuses disappear
- Doors close
- Prices go up
- Access ends
